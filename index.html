We'll be nestled next to the Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area and Kittatinny Mountain Ridge, full of hikes, trails, foliage lookouts and flowing streams and waterfalls.

Rules

｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡･ﾟﾟ･｡


We welcome you to be free and to help us create a freeing, feel-good environment that you want to return to.

Respect, kindness and community are key!

Say hi to people you may or may not know, kindness goes a long way. 


.・。.・゜✭・.・✫・゜・。.

Recycling

Please cooperate with Camp Hope’s firm recycling policy and place cans and bottles only in designated recycling bins. 


.・。.・゜✭・.・✫・゜・。.

Leave no trace


Treat Camp Hope like a home and leave it better than you found it!

This means respecting the land and cleaning up after yourself. Don’t leave anything around on the grounds or in your cabin after checking out. 


.・。.・゜✭・.・✫・゜・。.

Our dance floor

Being present and expressive on the dance floor is required. Please NO CAMERA, NO PHOTOS, NO SOCIALS, NO PHONES. And let GO!


.・。.・゜✭・.・✫・゜・。.

Smoking

You’ll find a koburomi (a Japanese-style portable ash pouch) in your welcome kit to help keep the party clean and beautiful.

Smoking tobacco is only allowed in the designated area on the Slow Motion Map. 


.・。.・゜✭・.・✫・゜・。.

Food storage

There are bears! All food (including snacks in sealed packaging) must be stored in the Dining Hall or in a sealed container in your cabin. 


.・。.・゜✭・.・✫・゜・。.

Camp Etiquette

Please be quiet in the cabins when people are sleeping. You can hang in the Dining Hall 24/7.

No tent camping, swimming in the lake, candles or speakers. 


.・。.・゜✭・.・✫・゜・。.

Safety

It takes all of us to have an enjoyable experience. Look out for each other, and if you feel uncertain about a situation, REACH OUT to a STAFF member. 


.・。.・゜✭・.・✫・゜・。.

What should I bring?
  • Comfortable clothing for day and night (layers, warm clothes, extra socks, waterproof gear if it rains)
  • Twin bedding for your cabin bunk, toiletries, towel and shower shoes
  • Reusable water bottle (we’ll have reusable cups available for $1)
  • Coolers, resealable containers and koozies for your beverages
  • Air-tight container or cooler for any snacks or food not stored in the Dining Hall
  • Picnic blanket, hammock, crafts, games, books, cooler or anything else for lounging
  • Yoga mat or blanket for free movement workshop on Saturday by Simeng Wang (LCAT-LP)
  • Flashlight or headlamp (we’re in the woods, and it’s beautifully dark), earplugs, sleep mask
  • Sunscreen and bug spray
  • Any medications or personal care items you may need

Don’t bring laptops, speakers or valuables. Unplug and enjoy the experience!

Can I bring my own food?
Yes! Instead of reserving a Meal Pass, you can reserve a space in the Dining Hall kitchen to prepare your own meals. Spaces are limited, please email us to request one.

There’s also a shared outdoor grill available to all guests, first-come first-served.

There are bears! Any food or snacks must be stored in the Dining Hall or in a sealed container / cooler.

Can I refund or transfer my ticket?
All ticket sales are final and non-refundable - this helps us cover fixed costs and keeps ticket prices as low as possible. However, we do offer ticket transfers.

You may transfer your ticket to someone else by emailing us at least 7 days before Slow Motion with the recipient’s full name and email address.

Availability may be limited, so we recommend requesting changes early.
